A social Austin food recommendation application. Find your new favorite restaurant - based on your location, tastes, and dietary needs - and share it with others.



Problem Statement



During the initial interview process, we found that people prefer to get restaurant recommendations from those in their social circle, however, they often have trouble remembering and acting on those restaurant recommendations.

We believe that by offering an app that tracks those recommendations, as well as the personal eating habits of users, we could not only find a way to help users find amazing eats based on their tastes and location, but offer an accessible way to recommend and review those dining experiences for others.




Survey Results

User Persona


When creating a user persona to design to, we focused on who would be the ideal user taking advantage of the app and who would most benefit from the services it provides. From there, we brought Trent Moore -hip, successfu, Electrical Engineer, to life. Trent is an transplant who moved to Austin for work. He is a self confessed “foodie” who likes to use the llocal food culture as a talking point for new acquaintances, and to plan impressive dates.




Empathy Map


Our next step was being congnizent of our persona’s thoughts, feelings, and experiences around the pain points we were solving for. We wanted to make sure we included aspects of our surveys and interviews to make sure he encompassed the main concerns and desires of our target market.
Our Top Priority: How do we advocate for our users and avoid designing for ourselves?


Based on research (survey, interviews, and in person user testing), Trent’s pains, gains, and thoughts revolve around:

- The overwhelming number of restaurant options in the city of Austin.
- Knowledge of the weight times, directions to, and location of restaurants.
- The lack of ability to keep track of and make recommendations.
- Finding quality options for friends, family, or dates with dietary restrictions.

Decision Flow



Journey Map



The current journey for our average user starts when they are in the negative, when they are hungry (or even hangry) and ends in a positive, when they have food and are enjoying the restaurant. There are many opportunities along the way to improve the users overall experience and the journey map allowed us to pinpoint these areas for growth.


For instance, we could anticipate the users needs by creating an algorithm that recommends a curated list of options in their area based on their tastes and budget. We could also provide directions, wait times, and menus for each location so that they can plan ahead in both traveling and ordering. There are also opportunites in utilizing filters to allow those with dietary restrictions to filter and find safe, delicious options.

To top it all off, we could create a social platform, allowing users to share their experiences with others, advocate for their favorite restaurants, and receive guidance from friends and family on where to go next.
